Indigenous Economic Intelligence, 2026 YTD

Private research dossier for Jake Carey. Scope: January 1 through July 15, 2026, Canada-wide, with deliberate depth on British Columbia and First Nations.

Evidence standard

  1. Build the event record before drafting synthesis.
  2. Separate occurrence date from effective date and announcement from execution.
  3. Use a primary source for the event whenever one is publicly available.
  4. Seek independent verification for material or contested claims.
  5. Label commentary, advocacy, allegation, and inference. None establishes a financial or legal fact on its own.
  6. Name First Nations, Inuit, and Métis governments and organizations distinctly and as they name themselves.
  7. Record unresolved terms, current stage, next catalyst, and confidence for every material event.
  8. Treat existing Nations briefs as discovery leads only.

Stage taxonomy

The ledger uses controlled stages including `discussion`, `proposal`, `consultation`, `MOU`, `option`, `legislation introduced`, `provincial ratification`, `legally effective`, `permit or approval`, `financing committed`, `financial close`, `transaction closed`, `construction`, `operational`, `litigation`, `judgment`, `implementation`, and `restructuring`.

Confidence taxonomy

  • High: primary evidence plus independent confirmation, or a self-authenticating public legal/legislative record.
  • Medium: credible primary evidence with incomplete commercial terms, or strong independent reporting without a second source.
  • Low: single-source claim, inaccessible underlying document, private transaction with material undisclosed terms, or unresolved allegation.

Final package

  • Long-form HTML report
  • Printable PDF report
  • Machine-readable JSON ledger
  • Machine-readable CSV ledger
  • Human-readable ledger
  • Weekday monitoring companion
  • Source gaps and confidence appendix
  • Indigenous language validation
